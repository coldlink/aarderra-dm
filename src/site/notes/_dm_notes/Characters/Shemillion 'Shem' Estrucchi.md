Shem is a brother in a small monastic order who follow the workings of a minor god of back doors, servants, anonymous charity, the unseen, and small mercies. Presumably this god is part of/subsumed within God of Justice, or is an aspect of him, but the justice they practice is more communal and less grand. They have an ascetic lifestyle of poverty and charity. Shem remembers only being part of the brotherhood, so he was probably given to the order as a baby, as the babies of people who can't support them often are in the places where the order is based. Shem has a gift for listening and noticing (think Brutha in Small Gods), and is young, innocent, and eager to please. His hamartia is that he's desperate to stay in everybody's good graces and terrified to disappoint. Last year, a powerful figure - perhaps a minister in the Church, or a member of a Florini family which supports his order - took notice of him and has been softly, subtly, without Shem knowing or realising, recruiting him to become a spy for them. Asking him to pay attention to particular confessions, to listen to what the servants or workers are talking about when he visits the sick, to go as an envoy of the poor and needy to opulent gatherings and see who is talking to whom... he thinks this is all just the work of his order, but really he's being slowly made into an instrument of someone else's power plays.
